Connecting a payment gateway lets your clients pay invoices in seconds directly from the invoice email or the online invoice page — no login, no phone calls, no checks in the mail. Once a payment is complete, eInvoices marks the invoice as **Paid** automatically and, if you have an accounting integration active, pushes the payment record to QuickBooks or Xero without any extra steps on your part.

## Supported Payment Methods by Gateway

| Gateway | Credit & Debit Cards | ACH / Bank Transfer | Digital Wallet |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stripe** | ✅ Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover | ✅ ACH Direct Debit (US) | ✅ Apple Pay, Google Pay |
| **PayPal** | ✅ Via PayPal Checkout | ❌ | ✅ PayPal Balance |
| **Square** | ✅ Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover | ❌ | ✅ Apple Pay, Google Pay |

<Note>
  You can connect **one payment gateway at a time**. If you switch gateways, disconnect the current one first. Existing paid invoices are not affected, and payment history is preserved in eInvoices.
</Note>

---

## How Clients Pay

When you send an invoice, the email includes a prominent **Pay Now** button. Clicking it opens a secure, hosted payment page that displays the invoice details and a payment form powered by your connected gateway. Clients do not need an eInvoices account to pay. The payment page is also linked from the invoice's online view, accessible via the share link on any invoice.

After a successful payment:
1. The gateway sends a real-time webhook to eInvoices.
2. eInvoices marks the invoice as **Paid** and records the transaction ID.
3. If an accounting integration is active, eInvoices pushes the payment to QuickBooks or Xero.
4. Your client receives an automatic payment confirmation email.

## Connect Your Payment Gateway

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Stripe">
    Stripe is connected using your Stripe **Publishable Key** and **Secret Key**. You generate these from the Stripe Dashboard.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Get Your Stripe API Keys">
        Log in to your [Stripe Dashboard](https://dashboard.stripe.com). In the left navigation, go to **Developers → API keys**. Copy the **Publishable key** and the **Secret key**. If you are in test mode, make sure to switch to **Live mode** before copying your keys for production use.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Integrations Settings">
        In your eInvoices dashboard, go to **Settings → Integrations**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select Stripe">
        Locate the **Stripe** card and click **Connect**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter Your API Keys">
        Paste your **Publishable key** into the first field and your **Secret key** into the second field.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save and Verify">
        Click **Save & Connect**. eInvoices makes a test call to the Stripe API to verify the keys. If the verification succeeds, the Stripe card shows a green **Connected** badge.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enable ACH (Optional)">
        To accept ACH Direct Debit payments, go to **Settings → Integrations → Stripe → Payment Methods** and toggle on **Bank Transfer (ACH)**. Your clients must be in the United States for ACH to appear as a payment option.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Your **Secret key** grants full access to your Stripe account. Never share it publicly or commit it to version control. If your secret key is ever exposed, immediately regenerate it from the Stripe Dashboard and update it in **Settings → Integrations → Stripe**.
    </Warning>

    ### Transaction Fees

    Stripe charges **2.9% + $0.30** per successful card transaction (standard US rate). ACH transfers are **0.8%**, capped at $5. These fees are charged by Stripe directly — eInvoices does not add any additional fee on top of Stripe's rate. For current pricing, refer to [Stripe's pricing page](https://stripe.com/pricing).

    ### Payouts

    By default, Stripe deposits funds to your linked bank account on a **2-day rolling basis**. You can adjust your payout schedule (daily, weekly, or manual) from within the Stripe Dashboard under **Balance → Manage payouts**.

    ### Refunds

    To issue a refund from eInvoices:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Payment Record">
        Go to the paid invoice and click **View Payment** to open the payment record.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Click Refund">
        Click **Issue Refund**. Enter the amount to refund (partial or full) and an optional reason.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm">
        Click **Confirm Refund**. eInvoices calls the Stripe API to process the refund. The invoice status reverts to **Unpaid** for a full refund, or shows a **Partially Refunded** badge for a partial refund.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    Stripe typically returns funds to the client's card within **5–10 business days**. Stripe's fees on the original transaction are not returned to you when you issue a refund.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="PayPal">
    PayPal is connected using a **Client ID** and **Secret** from your PayPal Developer dashboard. This is separate from your regular PayPal login credentials.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create a PayPal App">
        Log in to the [PayPal Developer Dashboard](https://developer.paypal.com/dashboard/). Under **My Apps & Credentials**, ensure you are on the **Live** tab, then click **Create App**. Give the app a name (for example, *eInvoices*) and click **Create App**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Copy Your Credentials">
        On the app detail page, copy the **Client ID** and the **Secret** (click **Show** to reveal the secret).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Integrations Settings">
        In your eInvoices dashboard, go to **Settings → Integrations**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select PayPal">
        Locate the **PayPal** card and click **Connect**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter Your Credentials">
        Paste your **Client ID** and **Secret** into the corresponding fields.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save and Verify">
        Click **Save & Connect**. eInvoices validates the credentials with the PayPal API. A green **Connected** badge confirms a successful connection.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Your PayPal **Secret** provides access to your PayPal business account. Keep it confidential. If it is ever compromised, log in to the PayPal Developer Dashboard and regenerate the secret immediately, then update it in **Settings → Integrations → PayPal**.
    </Warning>

    ### Supported Payment Methods

    Clients can pay using:
    - Any **major credit or debit card** through PayPal Checkout (the client does not need a PayPal account for card payments)
    - Their **PayPal balance**

    ### Transaction Fees

    PayPal charges **3.49% + $0.49** per transaction for PayPal Checkout (standard US rate). Rates vary by country and payment method. These fees are charged by PayPal — eInvoices does not add additional charges. For current pricing, refer to [PayPal's merchant fees page](https://www.paypal.com/us/webapps/mpp/merchant-fees).

    ### Payouts

    Payments are deposited to your linked PayPal Business account instantly. From there, you can transfer funds to your bank account manually or set up automatic transfers within your PayPal account settings. Transfer times depend on your bank and typically take **1–3 business days**.

    ### Refunds

    To issue a refund from eInvoices:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Payment Record">
        Navigate to the paid invoice and click **View Payment**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Click Refund">
        Click **Issue Refund**, enter the refund amount, and add an optional reason.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm">
        Click **Confirm Refund**. eInvoices calls the PayPal API to initiate the refund. Full refunds revert the invoice to **Unpaid**; partial refunds show a **Partially Refunded** badge.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    PayPal returns funds to the original payment method within **3–5 business days**. PayPal fees on the original transaction are not returned when you issue a refund.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Square">
    Square uses an OAuth 2.0 flow — no manual API key copying is required. eInvoices redirects you to Square's authorization page to grant access.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Integrations Settings">
        In your eInvoices dashboard, go to **Settings → Integrations**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select Square">
        Locate the **Square** card and click **Connect**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Sign In to Square">
        You are redirected to Square's authorization page. Sign in with your Square account credentials.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select Your Location">
        If your Square account has multiple locations, select the location where you want to receive payments, then click **Allow**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Return to eInvoices">
        Square redirects you back to the eInvoices dashboard. The Square card now shows a green **Connected** badge.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Your Square OAuth token is linked to a specific Square location. If you transfer ownership of your Square account or revoke permissions from Square's Developer Dashboard, the connection will break. Reconnect from **Settings → Integrations → Square** to restore it.
    </Warning>

    ### Supported Payment Methods

    Clients can pay using:
    - Any **major credit or debit card** (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover)
    - **Apple Pay** and **Google Pay** on supported devices and browsers

    ### Transaction Fees

    Square charges **2.9% + $0.30** per card transaction processed online (standard rate). These fees are charged by Square — eInvoices does not add any additional charges. For current pricing, refer to [Square's pricing page](https://squareup.com/us/en/payments/online-payments).

    ### Payouts

    Square deposits funds to your linked bank account on the **next business day** by default. You can enable **Instant Transfers** (for an additional fee) from within your Square Dashboard under **Account & Settings → Payout settings**.

    ### Refunds

    To issue a refund from eInvoices:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Payment Record">
        Navigate to the paid invoice and click **View Payment**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Click Refund">
        Click **Issue Refund**, enter the refund amount, and add an optional reason.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm">
        Click **Confirm Refund**. eInvoices calls the Square API to process the refund. The invoice status updates to **Unpaid** for a full refund, or **Partially Refunded** for a partial one.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    Square returns funds to the client's card within **2–7 business days** depending on their card issuer. Square fees on the original transaction are not returned when you issue a refund.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Transaction Fees: Summary

Gateway fees are set and charged by the payment provider — eInvoices does not take a cut of any transaction. The table below is a quick reference for standard US rates; check each provider's pricing page for rates in other countries.

| Gateway | Card Rate | ACH / Bank Transfer | Refund Handling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stripe | 2.9% + $0.30 | 0.8% (max $5) | Original fee not returned |
| PayPal | 3.49% + $0.49 | N/A | Original fee not returned |
| Square | 2.9% + $0.30 | N/A | Original fee not returned |

<Tip>
  Consider passing transaction fees to your clients using eInvoices' **Convenience Fee** setting under **Settings → Payments**. When enabled, a fee is added to the invoice total automatically when the client chooses to pay online, so your payout matches the invoice amount.
</Tip>

## Security and Compliance

eInvoices never stores raw card numbers. All payment data is handled directly by the gateway using their PCI-compliant hosted fields and tokenization. The **Pay Now** page served to your clients is loaded over HTTPS and uses iframe or redirect-based payment forms provided by Stripe, PayPal, or Square — your clients' card details never touch eInvoices servers.

<Note>
  If your business is subject to PCI DSS compliance requirements, using a hosted payment gateway through eInvoices qualifies you for the **SAQ A** self-assessment questionnaire — the simplest compliance path, since no card data is processed or stored on your own systems.
</Note>
