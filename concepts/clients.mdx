A client record in eInvoices is the central profile for every person or business you bill. Keeping your client records complete and up to date means eInvoices can pre-fill invoices accurately, surface the right payment history at a glance, and give clients seamless access to their own invoices through the client portal.

## What a Client Record Contains

When you create a client, you capture the following information.

### Contact Details

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Name** | The client's full name or business name, as it appears on invoices. |
| **Email** | Primary email address used for sending invoices and payment receipts. |
| **Phone** | Optional contact number, stored for your reference. |
| **Website** | Optional URL, useful for keeping a quick link to a business client's site. |

### Billing and Shipping Addresses

eInvoices stores separate **billing** and **shipping** addresses for each client.

- The **billing address** prints on every invoice sent to this client.
- The **shipping address** is available as an optional field on invoices where you need to specify a delivery destination.

Both addresses support multi-line entry and include country selection for international clients.

### Invoice Defaults

Setting defaults on a client record saves you time every time you raise a new invoice for that client.

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Default Currency** | The currency used when creating invoices for this client. Can be overridden per invoice. |
| **Default Payment Terms** | The standard payment window, e.g. *Net 30* or *Due on Receipt*. Sets the due date automatically when you create a new invoice. |

### Notes

The **Notes** field is a free-text area visible only to you and your team — clients never see it. Use it to record special billing instructions, account manager names, preferred contact times, or any other context that helps your team handle the account smoothly.

<Tip>
  If a client always pays in a specific currency or consistently requests extended payment terms, set those as their defaults so eInvoices applies them automatically to every new invoice.
</Tip>

## Clients and Invoice History

Every invoice you create for a client is linked directly to their record. From a client's profile page you can:

- **View all invoices** — See a chronological list of every invoice raised for that client, filterable by status (Draft, Sent, Overdue, Paid, Void).
- **View payment history** — See every payment recorded against the client's invoices, including the date, amount, payment method, and which invoice the payment was applied to.
- **Track outstanding balance** — The client profile shows the total amount currently owed across all open invoices.

This consolidated view means you never have to search across your full invoice list to understand the financial relationship with a specific client.

<Note>
  Payment history on a client record reflects all payments, including partial payments and refunds. The outstanding balance figure updates in real time as payments are recorded.
</Note>

## Client Portal

Every client has access to a dedicated **client portal** — a secure, branded web page where they can view and pay their invoices without needing to create an account.

### How it Works

1. When you send an invoice by email, the message includes a **View Invoice** button that links directly to that invoice in the client portal.
2. Once in the portal, the client can navigate to all their invoices — not just the one linked in the email.
3. The client can pay outstanding invoices online using any payment method you have enabled (credit card, bank transfer, etc.).
4. After payment, the portal updates immediately to reflect the new status.

### Portal Access

Client portal access is controlled by a **unique secure link** tied to the client's email address. There is no password to manage — the link itself serves as authentication.

<Warning>
  Because the portal link grants access to all of that client's invoices, treat it like a password. If a client believes their link has been compromised, you can regenerate it from their profile under **Client Actions → Regenerate Portal Link**. The old link becomes invalid immediately.
</Warning>

## Archiving vs Deleting Clients

When a client relationship ends, you have two options: **archive** or **delete**.

### Archiving a Client

Archiving hides the client from your active client list and prevents new invoices from being created for them, but preserves all their historical data.

- All past invoices remain intact and fully accessible.
- Payment history is retained for reporting and auditing purposes.
- The client can still access their portal and view historical invoices.
- You can **unarchive** a client at any time to resume billing them.

Archiving is the recommended approach for clients you are no longer actively working with.

### Deleting a Client

Deleting a client **permanently removes** their record and cannot be undone.

<Warning>
  You cannot delete a client who has any invoices — paid, unpaid, or voided — attached to their record. eInvoices requires you to delete or reassign those invoices first. For most situations, archiving is the safer and more practical choice.
</Warning>

Use deletion only for test records or duplicate entries created by mistake, where there is no meaningful invoice history to preserve.
