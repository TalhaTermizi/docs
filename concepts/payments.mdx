Payments in eInvoices connect money received to the invoices it settles. Whether a client pays online through your connected payment gateway or hands you a cheque, eInvoices gives you a consistent way to record, track, and reconcile every payment — and automatically keeps your invoice statuses up to date.

## Recording Payments

eInvoices supports two methods for recording a payment against an invoice: online payments processed through a payment gateway, and manual payments you record yourself.

### Online Payments

When you connect a payment gateway (such as Stripe or PayPal) to your eInvoices account, clients can pay directly from the **client portal** using a credit card, debit card, or other supported payment methods.

Online payments work as follows:

1. The client opens the invoice in their portal and clicks **Pay Now**.
2. They enter their payment details in the secure checkout hosted by the payment gateway.
3. The gateway processes the payment and sends a confirmation to eInvoices.
4. eInvoices automatically records the payment, updates the invoice status, and emails a receipt to the client.

You do not need to take any manual action — the entire process is handled end-to-end.

<Note>
  Payment gateway availability and transaction fees depend on the gateway you connect. eInvoices does not charge additional transaction fees beyond your gateway's own rates. See **Settings → Payment Gateways** to connect or configure a gateway.
</Note>

### Manual Payments

If a client pays you outside the online portal — by bank transfer, cheque, cash, or any other method — you record the payment manually in eInvoices.

**To record a manual payment:**
1. Open the invoice from your dashboard.
2. Click **Record Payment**.
3. Enter the payment date, amount, and payment method (Bank Transfer, Cheque, Cash, or Other).
4. Optionally add a reference number (such as a bank transaction ID or cheque number).
5. Click **Save Payment**.

eInvoices applies the payment to the invoice immediately and updates the invoice status accordingly.

<Tip>
  Use the reference number field for bank transfers — entering the transaction reference makes it much easier to match payments during reconciliation and respond to client queries.
</Tip>

## Partial Payments and Overpayments

### Partial Payments

A partial payment is any payment that covers less than the full outstanding balance on an invoice. eInvoices supports partial payments natively — simply record the amount actually received rather than the full invoice total.

After a partial payment is recorded:

- The invoice status remains **Sent** or **Overdue** (not Paid) until the full balance is settled.
- The invoice displays the **amount paid**, the **remaining balance**, and a full payment history log.
- You can record additional payments against the invoice at any time until the balance reaches zero.

Clients paying online through the portal can also choose to pay a partial amount by entering a custom figure in the payment amount field.

### Overpayments

If a payment recorded against an invoice exceeds the outstanding balance, eInvoices flags it as an **overpayment** and shows the surplus amount on the invoice.

You can handle an overpayment in two ways:

- **Apply the credit to a future invoice** — The surplus is stored as a credit on the client's account and can be applied when you create their next invoice.
- **Issue a refund** — Return the overpaid amount to the client. See [Refunds](#refunds) below.

## Payment Status

Every payment record in eInvoices carries one of four statuses.

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Pending** | The payment has been initiated (typically an online payment in progress) but not yet confirmed by the gateway. |
| **Completed** | The payment was successfully processed and funds have been confirmed. |
| **Failed** | The payment attempt was declined or encountered an error. The invoice balance is not affected. |
| **Refunded** | A full or partial refund has been issued against this payment. |

<Note>
  Manual payments you record in eInvoices always start with a **Completed** status, since you are confirming receipt of funds that have already arrived.
</Note>

## Payment Reconciliation

Reconciliation is the process of matching payments to invoices so that your books reflect exactly what has been paid and what remains outstanding.

In eInvoices, reconciliation is largely automatic:

- **Online payments** are reconciled the moment the gateway confirms the transaction. The invoice status updates immediately.
- **Manual payments** are reconciled when you record them. eInvoices deducts the payment amount from the invoice's outstanding balance.
- An invoice is marked **Paid** automatically as soon as the total of all recorded payments equals or exceeds the invoice total.

If you need to review reconciliation across multiple invoices, the **Payments** section of your dashboard shows every payment in chronological order, along with the invoice it was applied to and the resulting invoice status.

<Tip>
  Run the **Payment Summary** report (under **Reports → Payments**) to export a list of all payments received within a date range. This is useful for month-end reconciliation and accountant handoffs.
</Tip>

## Refunds

eInvoices lets you issue a full or partial refund against any completed payment.

### Full Refund

A full refund returns the entire payment amount to the client.

**To issue a full refund:**
1. Open the invoice and navigate to the **Payment History** section.
2. Click **Refund** next to the relevant payment.
3. Confirm the refund amount (pre-filled with the full payment amount).
4. Click **Issue Refund**.

For online payments processed through your connected gateway, eInvoices sends the refund instruction directly to the gateway — no separate action is needed in your gateway dashboard. For manual payments, eInvoices records the refund in your history but you must return the funds to the client through your own banking or cash process.

### Partial Refund

To issue a partial refund, follow the same steps as a full refund but change the amount in the refund confirmation screen to the specific amount you want to return.

After a refund is issued:

- The payment status changes to **Refunded**.
- The invoice outstanding balance is recalculated to reflect the returned amount.
- If the refund brings the paid amount below the invoice total, the invoice status reverts from **Paid** to **Sent** or **Overdue** as appropriate.

<Warning>
  Refunds processed through a payment gateway may take 5–10 business days to appear on the client's card or bank statement, depending on their bank. eInvoices records the refund immediately, but the client's funds arrive on the gateway's schedule.
</Warning>

## Payment Receipts

Every time a payment is successfully recorded — whether online or manual — eInvoices automatically sends a **payment receipt** to the client's email address.

The receipt email includes:

- The payment amount and date
- The payment method used
- The invoice number the payment was applied to
- The remaining balance (if the invoice is only partially paid)
- A PDF copy of the invoice attached to the email

You do not need to configure or trigger receipt emails manually. If you need to resend a receipt, open the payment record and click **Resend Receipt**.

<Note>
  Receipt emails are sent to the email address on the client's record at the time of payment. If a client's email address changes, update it in their client profile before recording future payments.
</Note>
