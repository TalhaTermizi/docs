Tax handling in eInvoices is built around named, reusable tax rates that you configure once and apply to invoices or individual line items. Whether you charge VAT, GST, sales tax, or multiple tax types simultaneously, eInvoices calculates the amounts automatically and keeps everything available for export when reporting time arrives.

<Note>
  eInvoices calculates and displays tax amounts on your invoices. It does not file tax returns, submit data to any tax authority, or provide tax advice. Consult a qualified accountant or tax adviser to ensure your tax configuration meets the legal requirements in your jurisdiction.
</Note>

## Creating Tax Rates

Before you can apply a tax to an invoice, you need to define it as a named tax rate.

**To create a tax rate:**
1. Go to **Settings → Tax Rates**.
2. Click **Add Tax Rate**.
3. Enter a **Name** — use something descriptive that you and your team will immediately recognise, such as `VAT 20%`, `GST 10%`, or `US Sales Tax 8.5%`.
4. Enter the **Rate** as a percentage (e.g. `20` for 20%).
5. Optionally add a short **Description** for internal reference.
6. Click **Save**.

You can create as many tax rates as your business needs — for example, a reduced rate and a standard rate under the same tax regime, or separate rates for different countries you invoice in.

### Setting a Default Tax Rate

If the majority of your invoices use the same tax rate, set it as your **default**. eInvoices pre-selects the default rate on every new line item, saving you from choosing it manually each time.

**To set a default:** On the **Tax Rates** settings page, click the three-dot menu next to the rate you want and select **Set as Default**.

<Tip>
  You can always override the default on a per-line-item or per-invoice basis. Setting a default is purely a convenience — it does not lock anything in.
</Tip>

## Applying Taxes

eInvoices gives you two ways to apply tax: per line item, or across the entire invoice.

### Per Line Item

Applying tax at the line-item level is the most flexible approach and is suitable when different products or services attract different tax rates — or when some items are tax-exempt.

When adding or editing a line item, select the appropriate tax rate from the **Tax** dropdown. eInvoices calculates the tax for that line as:

```
Line Tax = Quantity × Unit Price × Tax Rate
```

Each line item can carry a different tax rate, or none at all. The **Tax Total** on the invoice is the sum of taxes across all line items.

### Invoice-Wide Tax

If every item on an invoice attracts the same tax rate, you can apply a single tax rate to the whole invoice instead of setting it on each line item individually.

**To apply an invoice-wide tax:**
1. Open the invoice editor and scroll to the **Totals** section.
2. Click **Add Tax** under the subtotal.
3. Choose the tax rate from the dropdown.

Invoice-wide tax is calculated as a percentage of the subtotal (after any discount), and is added on top of any line-item taxes already applied.

<Warning>
  Mixing invoice-wide tax with per-line-item taxes on the same invoice can lead to items being taxed twice. Review the Tax Total carefully before sending if you use both approaches on the same invoice.
</Warning>

## Compound Taxes

A compound tax (sometimes called a "tax on tax") is a secondary tax calculated not on the original price, but on the price **after** a primary tax has already been applied.

**Example:**
- Item price: $100.00
- Primary tax (GST 10%): $10.00 → subtotal becomes $110.00
- Compound tax (PST 5%, applied to $110.00): $5.50
- **Total: $115.50**

This differs from two simple taxes stacked on the same base amount, where PST 5% would be calculated on $100.00 and equal $5.00.

**To create a compound tax rate:**
1. Go to **Settings → Tax Rates** and click **Add Tax Rate**.
2. Fill in the name and rate as normal.
3. Enable the **Compound Tax** toggle.
4. Click **Save**.

When you apply a compound tax to a line item alongside a standard tax, eInvoices automatically uses the post-primary-tax amount as the base for the compound calculation. eInvoices shows compound taxes clearly labelled in the invoice totals section so that clients can see exactly how the final amount was reached.

<Note>
  Compound taxes are only relevant in specific jurisdictions (notably Canada, for GST + PST combinations). Check with your accountant before enabling compound taxes to confirm they apply in your situation.
</Note>

## Tax-Exempt Clients and Invoices

Some clients may be legally exempt from certain taxes — for example, registered businesses claiming back VAT, charities, or government entities.

### Marking a Client as Tax-Exempt

1. Open the client's profile and click **Edit**.
2. Enable the **Tax Exempt** toggle.
3. Optionally enter the client's **Tax Exemption Number** for your records.
4. Click **Save**.

When you create a new invoice for a tax-exempt client, eInvoices automatically clears all tax rates from every line item. The invoice will display a "Tax Exempt" note in the totals section.

### Marking a Single Invoice as Tax-Exempt

If only one invoice for a client should be tax-free — rather than all their invoices — you can exempt a specific invoice without changing the client's profile:

1. Open the invoice editor.
2. In the **Invoice Settings** panel, enable **Tax Exempt**.
3. Remove any taxes already applied to line items.

<Tip>
  If a client provides a tax exemption certificate, upload a copy to their record under **Client Documents** so you have it on file if your records are ever audited.
</Tip>

## Tax Reports

eInvoices provides a built-in **Tax Summary** report that aggregates the tax you have collected across any date range. This report is the starting point for preparing your tax return with your accountant.

### Generating a Tax Summary

1. Go to **Reports → Tax Summary**.
2. Set the **Start Date** and **End Date** for the period you want to review.
3. Optionally filter by a specific tax rate if you want to isolate one tax type.
4. Click **Generate Report**.

The report displays:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Tax Rate** | The name of the tax rate (e.g. *VAT 20%*). |
| **Taxable Amount** | The total value of line items this tax was applied to. |
| **Tax Collected** | The total tax amount charged to clients. |
| **Invoice Count** | The number of invoices included in this row. |

### Exporting the Report

Click **Export CSV** or **Export PDF** to download the report in your preferred format. The CSV export is suitable for importing into accounting software or sharing with your accountant. The PDF export is formatted for filing or printing.

<Note>
  The Tax Summary report includes only **paid** invoices by default, since those represent tax you have actually collected. Use the **Include Unpaid** toggle to expand the report to cover all sent invoices — useful if you operate on an accrual basis.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Tax laws and filing requirements vary significantly by country, region, and business type. eInvoices provides tax collection and reporting tools to help you track what you owe, but it is your responsibility — and ideally your accountant's — to file the correct returns with the appropriate tax authority on time.
</Warning>
