eInvoices provides a suite of financial reports that give you a clear picture of your business's billing activity — from total revenue collected down to individual invoice status changes. Every report can be filtered by date range and client, and exported as CSV or PDF for accounting, tax filing, or client-facing summaries. Navigate to **Reports** in the left sidebar to access all available reports.

---

## Reports overview

| Report | What it shows | Export formats |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue Report** | Total invoiced, paid, and outstanding — filterable by date and client | CSV, PDF |
| **Aged Receivables** | Outstanding invoices grouped by how overdue they are | CSV, PDF |
| **Tax Summary** | Tax collected by rate across a date range | CSV |
| **Invoice Activity** | All invoice status changes with timestamps | CSV, PDF |

## Revenue report

The Revenue report gives you a high-level summary of your invoicing performance over any time period. Use it to understand how much you've billed, how much has been collected, and how much is still outstanding.

### What it shows

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Total Invoiced** | Sum of all invoice totals in the selected period, regardless of payment status |
| **Total Paid** | Sum of all fully paid invoice amounts in the period |
| **Total Outstanding** | Unpaid and partially paid invoice balances (Total Invoiced minus Total Paid) |
| **Average Invoice Value** | Total Invoiced divided by the number of invoices issued |
| **Collection Rate** | Total Paid as a percentage of Total Invoiced |

Below the summary metrics, a table lists every invoice in the period with its number, client, issue date, due date, amount, and status.

### Filters

- **Date range** — Filter by issue date or due date. Choose a preset (This Month, Last Month, This Quarter, This Year, Last Year) or enter a custom from/to date.
- **Client** — Narrow the report to a single client or leave blank to show all clients.
- **Status** — Filter by invoice status: All, Paid, Unpaid, Overdue, or Partial.

### How to run the Revenue report

1. Go to **Reports → Revenue**.
2. Set your **Date Range** and any other filters.
3. Click **Run Report**.
4. Review the summary cards and the invoice table.
5. Click **Export** to download the report (see [Exporting reports](#export-any-report)).

<Tip>
  Run the Revenue report filtered to **Last Month** at the beginning of each month for a clean monthly billing summary you can share with your accountant.
</Tip>

## Aged receivables

The Aged Receivables report shows all unpaid invoices grouped by how overdue they are. This is a standard accounting report used to prioritize collections and assess credit risk.

### Aging buckets

| Bucket | Description |
|---|---|
| **Current** | Invoices not yet due |
| **1–30 days** | Up to 30 days past the due date |
| **31–60 days** | 31 to 60 days past due |
| **61–90 days** | 61 to 90 days past due |
| **90+ days** | More than 90 days past due — highest priority for follow-up |

### How to read the report

The report is presented in two views:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Summary view">
    A table with one row per client, showing the outstanding balance split across each aging bucket. This gives you a quick scan of which clients owe the most and how overdue their balances are.

    | Client | Current | 1–30 days | 31–60 days | 61–90 days | 90+ days | Total |
    |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
    | Acme Corp | $0 | $1,200 | $0 | $0 | $0 | $1,200 |
    | Bright Studio | $2,500 | $0 | $750 | $0 | $400 | $3,650 |
    | Metro Events | $0 | $0 | $0 | $500 | $1,100 | $1,600 |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Detail view">
    Toggle to **Detail View** to expand each client row and see the individual invoices that make up each aging bucket. Each invoice row shows the invoice number, issue date, due date, original amount, and outstanding balance (useful for partially paid invoices).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### How to run the Aged Receivables report

1. Go to **Reports → Aged Receivables**.
2. Select the **As Of Date** — the report calculates aging relative to this date (defaults to today).
3. Optionally filter by **Client**.
4. Click **Run Report**.
5. Use the **Summary / Detail** toggle to switch views.
6. Click **Export** to download.

<Warning>
  The Aged Receivables report reflects balances **as of the selected date**, not the current date. If you export the report and open it later, the aging buckets in the export are frozen to the date you ran the report.
</Warning>

## Tax summary report

The Tax Summary report calculates the total tax you've collected across all paid invoices in a given period, broken down by tax rate. Use it to prepare VAT returns, sales tax filings, or any other tax reporting obligation.

### What it shows

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Tax Rate** | The tax rate (e.g., `20%`, `5%`, `0%`) |
| **Tax Name** | The label you've given the rate (e.g., `Standard VAT`, `Reduced Rate`) |
| **Taxable Amount** | The pre-tax subtotal to which this rate was applied |
| **Tax Collected** | The total tax amount collected at this rate |
| **Invoice Count** | Number of invoices that included this tax rate |

The report totals all rows at the bottom so you can see your aggregate tax liability at a glance.

### Filters

- **Date range** — Filter by the invoice issue date or payment date.
- **Tax status** — Choose **All invoices** or **Paid invoices only**. For most tax authorities, you'll want **Paid invoices only** (cash basis) or **All invoices** (accrual basis).
- **Client** — Optionally narrow the report to a single client.

### How to run the Tax Summary report

1. Go to **Reports → Tax Summary**.
2. Set your **Date Range** to match your tax period (e.g., quarterly or annual).
3. Select **Paid invoices only** or **All invoices** based on your accounting method.
4. Click **Run Report**.
5. Click **Export as CSV** to download for your accountant or tax software.

<Note>
  The Tax Summary report is only available as a CSV export — it is not currently exportable as a PDF. If you need a formatted PDF version, export the CSV and import it into your spreadsheet software.
</Note>

<Tip>
  If you use multiple tax rates across different products or services, run the Tax Summary report and share the CSV directly with your accountant at the end of each tax period to save time on manual calculations.
</Tip>

## Invoice activity report

The Invoice Activity report is a full audit trail of every status change across all your invoices. Use it to investigate payment timelines, diagnose delivery issues, or provide a compliance record.

### What it shows

Every row in the activity log represents a single event on a single invoice:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Timestamp** | Date and time the event occurred (shown in your account timezone) |
| **Invoice** | Invoice number, linked to the invoice detail view |
| **Client** | The client billed on that invoice |
| **Event** | The status change or action (see event types below) |
| **Details** | Additional context, such as the recipient email or payment method |
| **User** | The team member who triggered the event (for manual actions) |

### Event types

| Event | Trigger |
|---|---|
| `Created` | Invoice was created |
| `Sent` | Invoice was emailed to the client |
| `Viewed` | Client opened the hosted invoice page |
| `Payment received` | A full or partial payment was recorded |
| `Marked as paid` | Invoice was manually marked as paid |
| `Reminder sent` | An automatic or manual reminder was dispatched |
| `Overdue` | Invoice passed its due date without full payment |
| `Voided` | Invoice was voided |
| `Edited` | Invoice fields were changed |

### Filters

- **Date range** — Filter events by timestamp.
- **Client** — Show events for a single client.
- **Invoice** — Show events for a single invoice number.
- **Event type** — Filter to specific events (e.g., show only `Reminder sent` events).

### How to run the Invoice Activity report

1. Go to **Reports → Invoice Activity**.
2. Apply any filters to narrow the results.
3. Click **Run Report**.
4. Click **Export** to download.

## Export any report

Every report in eInvoices can be exported for use in external tools, shared with an accountant, or archived for compliance purposes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run the report">
    Apply your filters and click **Run Report** to generate the results.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Export">
    Click the **Export** button (top-right of the results panel). A dropdown shows the available formats for the current report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a format">
    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="CSV">
        Downloads a comma-separated values file compatible with Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, Apple Numbers, and most accounting software. Column headers match the field names shown in the report.

        Use CSV when you need to:
        - Import data into accounting software (e.g., Xero, QuickBooks)
        - Manipulate data in a spreadsheet
        - Share raw data with your accountant
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="PDF">
        Downloads a formatted, print-ready PDF with your business name, logo, report name, date range, and all data rows. Ideal for presenting reports to stakeholders or filing printed records.

        Use PDF when you need to:
        - Share a polished summary with a client or investor
        - Print or archive a report for compliance
        - Attach a report to an email or proposal

        <Note>
          The Tax Summary report is only available as CSV. See [Tax Summary report](#tax-summary-report) for details.
        </Note>
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download the file">
    The file downloads automatically to your browser's default download location. Large reports (over 1,000 rows) may take a few seconds to generate. A progress indicator appears while eInvoices prepares the file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you regularly export the same report with the same filters, save the filtered report URL as a bookmark. Your filter settings are stored in the URL, so reopening the bookmark re-applies them automatically.
</Tip>
