Once you've created an invoice, eInvoices gives you several ways to deliver it to your client — by email directly from the platform, via a shareable payment link, or by recording a delivery you made outside eInvoices. Every method updates the invoice status automatically so your records stay accurate.

## Send by email

Sending an invoice by email is the most common delivery method. eInvoices composes the email for you and attaches the PDF automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Send dialog">
    From the invoice detail view, click **Send Invoice**. If the invoice is still a draft, eInvoices finalizes it before opening the send dialog.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the recipient address">
    The **To** field defaults to the email address stored on the client's record. You can:

    - Edit the address directly in the field for a one-off change (this doesn't update the client record).
    - Add CC recipients by clicking **+ Add CC** and entering additional addresses separated by commas.

    <Tip>
      To permanently update a client's email address, go to **Clients**, open the client record, and edit the **Email** field there.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the subject line">
    The subject defaults to:

    ```
    Invoice {invoice_number} from {your_business_name}
    ```

    Edit it freely. Variables like `{invoice_number}` resolve to their actual values before the email is sent.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Compose the message body">
    The message body pre-fills with your default email template (configured in **Settings → Email Templates**). You can edit the body directly in the dialog for this send only, or update your default template to change it for all future invoices.

    The following variables are available in the message body:

    | Variable | Resolves to |
    |---|---|
    | `{client_name}` | The client's full name or business name |
    | `{invoice_number}` | The invoice number (e.g., `INV-0042`) |
    | `{amount_due}` | The total amount due including currency symbol |
    | `{due_date}` | The formatted due date (e.g., `15 August 2025`) |
    | `{payment_link}` | A clickable URL to the hosted invoice payment page |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Toggle PDF attachment">
    The **Attach PDF** toggle is on by default. The client receives the invoice as both an inline HTML view (via the payment link) and a downloadable PDF attachment.

    Turn the toggle off if you're sending to a client whose email provider strips attachments or if you prefer the client to view the invoice online only.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send the invoice">
    Click **Send Now** to dispatch the email immediately. The invoice status changes from `Draft` to `Sent`, and a timestamp is recorded under **Activity** on the invoice detail view.

    <Note>
      eInvoices tracks when the client opens the email and views the invoice. Watch for the `Viewed` status badge to know your client has seen it.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Share a payment link

Every invoice has a unique, shareable payment link that opens a hosted invoice page in the client's browser — no login required. Use this link to deliver invoices over messaging apps, in proposals, or anywhere email isn't ideal.

### Copy the payment link

1. Open the invoice detail view.
2. Click **Copy Payment Link** (below the **Send Invoice** button).
3. Paste the link anywhere — a chat message, proposal document, client portal, or email you compose yourself.

The hosted invoice page shows:
- Your logo and brand colors
- All invoice details: line items, taxes, discount, total, and due date
- A **Pay Now** button (if online payments are enabled in **Settings → Payments**)
- A **Download PDF** button

<Note>
  Payment links remain active as long as the invoice is open. Once the invoice is marked as paid or void, the link shows a "This invoice is no longer active" message.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Payment links do not require authentication. Anyone with the link can view the invoice. Avoid posting payment links in public channels.
</Warning>

## Mark an invoice as sent manually

If you delivered an invoice outside eInvoices — for example, you handed a printed copy to a client or sent the PDF from your personal email — record the delivery so your eInvoices records stay accurate.

1. Open the invoice detail view.
2. Click the **⋮** (More actions) menu.
3. Select **Mark as Sent**.
4. Confirm the action in the dialog. eInvoices updates the invoice status to `Sent` and logs the event with the current timestamp.

<Note>
  Marking an invoice as sent manually does **not** send any email. It only updates the invoice status in eInvoices.
</Note>

## Resend an invoice or send a reminder

### Resend the invoice email

To resend the original invoice email — for example, if your client says they didn't receive it:

1. Open the invoice detail view.
2. Click **Resend Invoice**.
3. Confirm or edit the recipient, subject, and message body.
4. Click **Send Now**.

Resending doesn't create a new invoice or change the invoice number. The activity log records each delivery separately.

### Send a manual reminder

To send a one-off payment reminder for a specific invoice:

1. Open the invoice detail view.
2. Click **Send Reminder**.
3. The reminder dialog pre-fills with your default reminder template. Edit the subject or message if needed.
4. Click **Send Reminder**.

The reminder appears in the **Reminders** tab of the invoice detail view with a sent timestamp.

<Tip>
  For automated reminders that trigger N days before or after the due date, see [Payment Reminders](/guides/payment-reminders).
</Tip>

## What your client sees

When a client receives an invoice email from eInvoices, here's what their experience looks like:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Email inbox">
    The client receives a branded email containing:

    - Your business name, logo, and reply-to address
    - A summary line: invoice number, amount due, and due date
    - The message body you composed
    - A prominent **View & Pay Invoice** button linking to the hosted invoice page
    - The PDF as an attachment (if the Attach PDF toggle was on)

    The email is sent from `invoices@einvoices.io` with your business name as the display sender (e.g., `Acme Design via eInvoices`). Replies go to your registered business email address.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Hosted invoice page">
    Clicking **View & Pay Invoice** opens a mobile-responsive hosted page showing:

    | Section | Contents |
    |---|---|
    | **Header** | Your logo, business name, and contact details |
    | **Invoice details** | Invoice number, issue date, due date |
    | **Bill To** | Client name and billing address |
    | **Line items** | Description, quantity, unit price, tax, and amount per row |
    | **Totals** | Subtotal, discount (if any), total tax, and amount due |
    | **Notes** | Your payment instructions or thank-you message |
    | **Pay Now** | Payment button (credit card, bank transfer, etc.) |
    | **Download PDF** | Link to download the invoice as a PDF |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="After payment">
    After your client completes payment:

    - The hosted page shows a **Payment Received** confirmation with the payment date and amount.
    - The **Pay Now** button is replaced by a receipt download link.
    - You receive an email notification and the invoice status updates to `Paid` in eInvoices.
    - The client receives an automatic payment receipt email.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Invoice status reference

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Draft` | Invoice created but not sent |
| `Sent` | Invoice emailed or marked as sent |
| `Viewed` | Client has opened the hosted invoice page |
| `Partial` | Client has paid part of the invoice total |
| `Paid` | Invoice fully paid |
| `Overdue` | Past due date with no full payment |
| `Void` | Invoice cancelled; no longer payable |
