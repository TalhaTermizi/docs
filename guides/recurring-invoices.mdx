A recurring invoice is a reusable billing template that eInvoices uses to automatically generate new invoices on a schedule you define. Instead of manually recreating the same invoice every month for a retainer client or subscription service, you configure the template once and let eInvoices handle the rest. You can choose to auto-send the generated invoice or save it as a draft for review before it goes out.

## How recurring invoices work

When you set up a recurring invoice, eInvoices stores a **template** — not a live invoice. On each scheduled date, eInvoices creates a new invoice from the template, assigns it the next available invoice number, sets a fresh issue date and due date, and either sends it immediately or saves it as a draft, depending on your auto-send setting.

Generated invoices are independent of the template. Editing the template later affects only future invoices — invoices that have already been generated are not changed.

## Set up a recurring invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the recurring invoice builder">
    In the left sidebar, click **Invoices**, then click the dropdown arrow next to **New Invoice** and select **New Recurring Invoice**. Alternatively, open any existing invoice and choose **Make Recurring** from the **⋮** (More actions) menu to pre-fill the template with that invoice's details.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a client">
    In the **Bill To** field, search for and select the client this recurring invoice is for. All generated invoices will be billed to this client.

    <Tip>
      You can only assign one client per recurring invoice template. To bill multiple clients on the same schedule, create a separate recurring invoice for each.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add line items, taxes, and discounts">
    Fill in the line items exactly as you would for a standard invoice — description, quantity, unit price, and tax rate for each row. Apply a fixed or percentage discount if needed.

    These line items appear on every generated invoice. If prices change over time, edit the template and the new rates will apply to future invoices only.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the recurring schedule">
    Under **Recurring Schedule**, set the following fields:

    | Field | Options / Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Frequency** | Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annually |
    | **Start Date** | The date the first invoice is generated; defaults to today |
    | **End Condition** | Choose **No end date**, **End after N occurrences**, or **End on specific date** |

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Weekly">
        An invoice is generated every 7 days from the start date. Useful for weekly contractors or short-cycle service agreements.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Monthly">
        An invoice is generated on the same day each month (e.g., every 1st). If the start date is the 31st, eInvoices uses the last day of the month for shorter months.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Quarterly">
        An invoice is generated every three months from the start date. Suitable for quarterly retainers or subscription services billed every quarter.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Annually">
        An invoice is generated once per year on the anniversary of the start date. Ideal for annual service agreements or yearly licensing fees.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    **Example — 12-month monthly retainer:**
    - Frequency: Monthly
    - Start Date: 1 August 2025
    - End Condition: End after **12** occurrences

    eInvoices will generate invoices on 1 Aug, 1 Sep, 1 Oct … through 1 Jul 2026, then stop.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the due date rule">
    Choose how the due date is calculated for each generated invoice:

    - **Net-7 / Net-15 / Net-30 / Net-60** — Due N days after the issue date
    - **Custom days** — Enter any number of days
    - **Same as issue date** — Payment due on receipt

    The due date rule is applied fresh each time an invoice is generated, so if the issue date is 1 August and the rule is Net-30, the due date is 31 August.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose auto-send or draft">
    Under **When a new invoice is generated**, select one of the following:

    - **Auto-send** — eInvoices emails the generated invoice to the client automatically using your default email template. No action required from you.
    - **Save as draft** — The invoice is created with `Draft` status and waits for you to review and send it manually.

    <Note>
      If you choose **Auto-send**, make sure the client record has a valid email address before activating the recurring invoice. eInvoices validates this when you save the template.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add notes and payment terms">
    Enter any notes or payment terms to include on every generated invoice. These can reference the service period or subscription details your client needs to see.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the recurring invoice">
    Click **Save Recurring Invoice**. The template is now active. You can find it under **Invoices → Recurring** at any time.

    eInvoices confirms the next scheduled generation date. On that date, the first invoice is created automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Changes you make to a recurring invoice template apply to **future** generated invoices only. Invoices that have already been generated are independent documents — editing the template does not modify them retroactively.
</Note>

## Edit a recurring invoice template

1. Navigate to **Invoices → Recurring**.
2. Click the template name to open it.
3. Click **Edit Template**.
4. Make your changes — update line items, prices, schedule, or auto-send settings.
5. Click **Save Changes**.

A confirmation banner reminds you that only future invoices will reflect the changes. Any invoices already generated remain unchanged.

<Warning>
  If you need to correct an invoice that has already been generated, open that specific invoice from the **Invoices** list and edit it directly. Changes there affect only that single invoice.
</Warning>

## Pause a recurring invoice

Pausing a recurring invoice stops new invoices from being generated without deleting the template. This is useful during a project pause, a client dispute, or a seasonal gap in services.

1. Go to **Invoices → Recurring**.
2. Find the template and click the **⋮** menu.
3. Select **Pause**.

The template status changes to `Paused` and no invoices are generated while it's paused. The scheduled dates are not preserved — when you resume, the next invoice generates based on the original frequency from the resume date onward.

To **resume** a paused template:
1. Open the paused template.
2. Click **Resume**.
3. Confirm the new next-generation date in the dialog.

---

## Cancel a recurring invoice

Cancelling permanently stops the recurring invoice. This action cannot be undone.

1. Go to **Invoices → Recurring**.
2. Click the **⋮** menu on the template.
3. Select **Cancel Recurring Invoice**.
4. Type `CANCEL` in the confirmation field and click **Confirm**.

The template status changes to `Cancelled`. All previously generated invoices remain intact and are unaffected.

<Tip>
  If there's any chance you'll restart billing for this client, consider pausing instead of cancelling. A paused template is easier to reactivate than recreating a cancelled one from scratch.
</Tip>

## View generated invoices

Every invoice generated from a recurring template is linked back to its source.

1. Open the recurring invoice template from **Invoices → Recurring**.
2. Scroll to the **Generated Invoices** section.

You'll see a table listing each generated invoice with its number, issue date, due date, amount, and current status (`Draft`, `Sent`, `Paid`, `Overdue`, etc.). Click any row to open that invoice directly.

## Recurring invoice status reference

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Active` | Generating invoices on schedule |
| `Paused` | Temporarily stopped; can be resumed |
| `Cancelled` | Permanently stopped; no new invoices will be generated |
| `Completed` | All scheduled occurrences have been generated |
