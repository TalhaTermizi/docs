Late payments are one of the most common friction points in managing invoices. eInvoices solves this with automatic payment reminders that trigger on a schedule you define — before an invoice is due, on the due date itself, and after the due date if it goes unpaid. You can customize the email template, override reminder settings for individual invoices, and send one-off manual reminders whenever needed.

## How automatic reminders work

Automatic reminders are global rules that apply to all your sent invoices by default. Each rule specifies a **trigger time** relative to the invoice due date and sends a reminder email to the client if the invoice is still unpaid at that point. You can define multiple rules — for example, a nudge 3 days before, a notice on the due date, and a follow-up 7 days after.

---

## Configure automatic reminder rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Reminder Settings">
    In the left sidebar, click **Settings**, then select **Payment Reminders**. The reminder rules panel shows any rules you've already created.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a new rule">
    Click **+ Add Reminder Rule**. A rule configuration row appears with three fields:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Timing** | Choose **Before due date**, **On due date**, or **After due date** |
    | **Days** | Number of days before or after the due date (not shown for "On due date") |
    | **Template** | The email template to use for this reminder (see [Customize reminder templates](#customize-reminder-email-templates)) |

    **Common rule configurations:**

    | Rule | When it fires |
    |---|---|
    | 7 days before due date | A gentle heads-up to clients with sufficient lead time |
    | 3 days before due date | A closer nudge so clients can arrange payment |
    | On due date | A "payment due today" notice |
    | 3 days after due date | A polite follow-up for clients who missed the deadline |
    | 7 days after due date | A firmer overdue notice |
    | 14 days after due date | A final notice before escalation |

    <Tip>
      Start with two or three rules to avoid overwhelming clients. A common setup is: **3 days before**, **on due date**, and **7 days after**.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable reminder rules">
    After adding your rules, make sure the **Automatic Reminders** master toggle at the top of the page is turned **On**. When this toggle is off, no automatic reminders fire regardless of individual rule settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your rules">
    Click **Save Changes**. Your rules are now active for all new and existing open invoices, unless a specific invoice has reminders disabled (see [Per-invoice overrides](#per-invoice-overrides)).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Automatic reminders only fire for invoices with `Sent` or `Overdue` status. They do not fire for `Draft`, `Paid`, or `Void` invoices.
</Note>

## Customize reminder email templates

Each reminder rule can use a different email template — for example, a friendly tone before the due date and a more direct tone after. You can customize the subject line and body of every template.

### Edit a template

1. Go to **Settings → Payment Reminders**.
2. Click **Manage Templates** (top-right of the page).
3. Click **Edit** next to the template you want to change, or click **+ New Template** to create one from scratch.
4. Edit the **Subject** and **Body** fields.
5. Click **Save Template**.

### Available variables

Use these variables anywhere in the subject or body. eInvoices replaces them with the actual values when the reminder is sent.

| Variable | Resolves to |
|---|---|
| `{{client_name}}` | The client's name or business name |
| `{{invoice_number}}` | The invoice number (e.g., `INV-0042`) |
| `{{amount_due}}` | The total outstanding amount with currency symbol |
| `{{due_date}}` | The formatted due date (e.g., `15 August 2025`) |
| `{{payment_link}}` | A direct link to the hosted invoice payment page |
| `{{your_business_name}}` | Your registered business name |
| `{{days_overdue}}` | Number of days the invoice is past due (for post-due templates) |

### Example templates

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Before due date">
    **Subject:**
    ```
    Reminder: Invoice {{invoice_number}} due on {{due_date}}
    ```

    **Body:**
    ```
    Hi {{client_name}},

    This is a friendly reminder that invoice {{invoice_number}} for {{amount_due}} 
    is due on {{due_date}}.

    You can view and pay your invoice here:
    {{payment_link}}

    Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have any questions.

    Thanks,
    {{your_business_name}}
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="On due date">
    **Subject:**
    ```
    Payment due today — Invoice {{invoice_number}}
    ```

    **Body:**
    ```
    Hi {{client_name}},

    Invoice {{invoice_number}} for {{amount_due}} is due today.

    Pay now to keep your account current:
    {{payment_link}}

    Thank you,
    {{your_business_name}}
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="After due date">
    **Subject:**
    ```
    Overdue: Invoice {{invoice_number}} — {{days_overdue}} days past due
    ```

    **Body:**
    ```
    Hi {{client_name}},

    Invoice {{invoice_number}} for {{amount_due}} was due on {{due_date}} and 
    remains unpaid. Your account is now {{days_overdue}} days overdue.

    Please arrange payment at your earliest convenience:
    {{payment_link}}

    If you've already sent payment, please disregard this notice. 
    Contact us if you have any questions.

    {{your_business_name}}
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  Always include `{{payment_link}}` in your reminder templates. Without it, clients have no direct path to pay and are more likely to delay.
</Warning>

## Per-invoice overrides

Sometimes you need to suppress reminders for a specific invoice — for example, a client on a payment plan, a disputed invoice, or a long-standing relationship where reminders feel inappropriate.

### Disable reminders for a specific invoice

1. Open the invoice detail view.
2. Click the **⋮** (More actions) menu.
3. Select **Reminder Settings**.
4. Toggle **Send automatic reminders** to **Off**.
5. Click **Save**.

The invoice is now excluded from all automatic reminder rules. The global reminder settings are not affected, and all your other invoices continue to receive reminders as configured.

### Re-enable reminders

Follow the same steps and toggle **Send automatic reminders** back to **On**. Future scheduled reminders will resume for that invoice.

<Note>
  Disabling reminders on an invoice does not cancel reminders that have already been sent. It only prevents future scheduled reminders from firing.
</Note>

## Send a manual reminder

If you want to send a one-off reminder outside the automatic schedule — for example, after a phone call with a client or when an invoice becomes significantly overdue — you can send a manual reminder from the invoice detail view.

1. Open the invoice from the **Invoices** list.
2. Click **Send Reminder**.
3. In the dialog, confirm or edit:
   - **To** — Recipient email address (defaults to the client's email)
   - **Subject** — Pre-filled from your default reminder template; editable
   - **Body** — Pre-filled; edit freely for a personal touch
4. Click **Send Reminder**.

The reminder is dispatched immediately and recorded in the **Reminder History** tab of the invoice.

<Tip>
  Personalizing the message body for manual reminders — adding context about the project or referencing a previous conversation — is often more effective than the standard template.
</Tip>

## View reminder history

Every reminder sent for an invoice — automatic or manual — is logged in the **Reminder History** tab on the invoice detail view.

The history table shows:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Date & Time** | When the reminder was sent |
| **Type** | `Automatic` or `Manual` |
| **Trigger** | The rule that fired it (e.g., "3 days after due date") or "Manual" |
| **Recipient** | The email address the reminder was sent to |
| **Status** | `Delivered`, `Opened`, or `Failed` |

Use the reminder history to verify that reminders have been sent, check whether a client has opened them, and decide when to follow up through other channels.

<Note>
  Email open tracking requires that the client's email client loads remote images. Some clients (such as Apple Mail with Mail Privacy Protection enabled) may not accurately report `Opened` status.
</Note>
