Creating an invoice in eInvoices takes just a few minutes. The invoice builder walks you through every field — from selecting a client and adding line items to applying discounts and previewing the finished PDF — so you can send polished, accurate invoices every time.

## Create a new invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Invoices → New Invoice">
    In the left sidebar, click **Invoices**, then click the **New Invoice** button in the top-right corner. The invoice builder opens in a full-page editor.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select or create a client">
    In the **Bill To** section, start typing a client name in the search field. eInvoices searches your client list as you type.

    - **Existing client** — Select the client from the dropdown. Their billing address and default currency populate automatically.
    - **New client** — Click **+ Create client**, fill in the name, email, and billing address in the side panel, then click **Save**. The new client is selected immediately.

    <Tip>
      You can manage client details later from the **Clients** section without losing any draft invoices linked to them.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set invoice number, issue date, and due date">
    eInvoices auto-increments the **Invoice Number** based on your last invoice. You can override it manually if you follow a custom numbering scheme.

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Invoice Number** | Unique identifier for this invoice (e.g., `INV-0042`) |
    | **Issue Date** | The date the invoice is created; defaults to today |
    | **Due Date** | The payment deadline; choose from net-7, net-15, net-30, net-60, or a custom date |
    | **Currency** | Inherited from the client; change it per invoice if needed |
    | **PO Number** | Optional purchase order reference for the client's records |

    <Note>
      Your default payment terms (e.g., net-30) are set in **Settings → Invoice Defaults** and applied automatically to every new invoice.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add line items">
    Click **+ Add Line Item** to add a row to the line-items table. Each row has four fields:

    | Column | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Description** | The product or service name; supports multi-line text |
    | **Qty** | Quantity (supports decimals, e.g., `2.5` hours) |
    | **Unit Price** | Price per unit in the invoice currency |
    | **Tax** | Select a saved tax rate or type a one-off percentage |

    The **Amount** column calculates automatically (`Qty × Unit Price`). Tax is applied per line item, letting you mix taxable and non-taxable items on the same invoice.

    Click the **⋮** menu on any row to duplicate or delete it. Drag rows by the handle on the left to reorder them.

    <Tip>
      Save frequently used items — such as hourly consulting rates or standard product SKUs — in **Settings → Products & Services** so you can add them with a single click.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply a discount (optional)">
    Below the line-items table, toggle **Add Discount** to reveal the discount field. Choose the discount type, then enter the value.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Fixed amount">
        Enter a flat currency amount to deduct from the subtotal (e.g., `50.00`). The discount is applied after all line-item amounts are summed but before tax.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Percentage">
        Enter a percentage between `0` and `100` (e.g., `10` for 10 %). eInvoices calculates the discount as a percentage of the pre-tax subtotal and shows the derived amount in grey.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    <Note>
      Discounts are applied to the overall subtotal, not to individual line items. If you need item-level discounts, adjust the unit price for that row directly.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add notes and payment terms">
    Use the two text areas at the bottom of the invoice to communicate extra information to your client.

    - **Notes** — Visible on the PDF invoice. Use this for project references, thank-you messages, or delivery instructions.
    - **Payment Terms** — Also visible on the PDF. Describe accepted payment methods, late-payment penalties, or bank transfer details.

    Both fields support plain text and preserve line breaks in the rendered PDF.

    <Tip>
      Save your standard payment terms in **Settings → Invoice Defaults** so they pre-fill on every new invoice automatically.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview the PDF">
    Click **Preview** (top-right of the editor) to open a live PDF render in a side panel. The preview updates every time you make a change, so you can verify layout, totals, and formatting before sending.

    Check the preview for:
    - Correct client name and billing address
    - Accurate line-item amounts and tax calculations
    - Discount applied and reflected in the total
    - Your logo and brand colors (configured in **Settings → Branding**)

    <Warning>
      If your logo appears blurry in the preview, upload a PNG or SVG at least 400 × 400 px in **Settings → Branding**.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save as Draft or Send immediately">
    When you're happy with the invoice, choose how to proceed:

    - **Save as Draft** — Saves the invoice without sending it. The invoice appears in **Invoices** with a `Draft` status badge and can be edited or sent later.
    - **Send Invoice** — Opens the send dialog where you confirm the recipient email, subject line, and message before dispatching. See [Send an Invoice](/guides/send-invoice) for full details.

    <Note>
      Draft invoices are never visible to your client. Only invoices with a `Sent` status have been delivered.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Duplicate an existing invoice

Duplicating is the fastest way to create a new invoice that shares the same client, line items, and terms as a previous one.

1. Open the invoice you want to copy from the **Invoices** list.
2. Click the **⋮** (More actions) menu in the top-right of the invoice detail view.
3. Select **Duplicate Invoice**.

eInvoices creates a new draft with:
- The same client, line items, taxes, discount, notes, and payment terms
- A new auto-incremented invoice number
- Today's date as the issue date
- A recalculated due date based on your default payment terms

Edit any field before saving or sending. The original invoice is not affected.

## Import line items from CSV

If your invoice contains many line items — such as a detailed project timesheet or a bulk product order — you can import them all at once using a CSV file instead of adding rows one by one.

### CSV format

Your CSV must use the following column headers in the first row:

| Column | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `description` | ✅ Yes | Line item name or description |
| `quantity` | ✅ Yes | Numeric quantity (e.g., `3` or `1.5`) |
| `unit_price` | ✅ Yes | Price per unit, without currency symbol (e.g., `250.00`) |
| `tax_rate` | ✅ Yes | Tax rate as a percentage (e.g., `20` for 20 %); use `0` for no tax |

**Example CSV:**

```csv
description,quantity,unit_price,tax_rate
Website design,1,1500.00,20
Logo design,1,500.00,20
Hosting setup,1,150.00,0
Content migration,8,75.00,20
```

### Import steps

1. In the invoice builder, click **Import Line Items** (above the line-items table).
2. Click **Choose File** and select your `.csv` file.
3. Review the parsed rows in the confirmation dialog — correct any errors highlighted in red.
4. Click **Import** to add all rows to the invoice.

<Warning>
  Importing line items **replaces** any existing rows on the invoice. Download a copy of your current line items first if you want to preserve them.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Prepare your CSV file with these columns and import it directly into the line items section.
</Tip>
