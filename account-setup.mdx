Your eInvoices account settings control everything your clients see on every invoice you send — from your company logo and brand colours to your default tax rate and the reply-to address on outbound emails. Taking a few minutes to configure these settings once means every invoice you create afterward is accurate, on-brand, and ready to send without manual adjustments.

<Note>
  Logo and branding changes apply to all **future** invoices. They do not retroactively update invoices that have already been sent or marked as paid. If you need to correct information on a sent invoice, create a revised version manually.
</Note>

To access your account settings, click **Settings** in the left sidebar of the eInvoices dashboard.

---

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Business Profile">
    Your business profile provides the foundational details that appear in the **From** block on every invoice. Keep this information accurate and up to date — it is also used for legal compliance in many jurisdictions.

    ### Company Information

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Company Name** | Your registered business name or trading name, exactly as it should appear on invoices. |
    | **Business Address** | Your full mailing or registered address, including street, city, state/province, postal code, and country. |
    | **Phone Number** | An optional contact number displayed on the invoice footer. |
    | **Website** | Your business website URL (for example, `https://www.yourcompany.com`). This appears as a clickable link on digital invoices. |

    ### Uploading Your Logo

    Your logo appears prominently in the top section of every invoice. Follow these guidelines to ensure it renders correctly:

    - **Accepted formats:** PNG or JPG
    - **Maximum file size:** 2 MB
    - **Recommended dimensions:** At least 300 × 100 px at 72 dpi or higher
    - **Background:** Use a transparent background (PNG) for the cleanest look on all invoice templates

    To upload your logo:

    1. Navigate to **Settings → Business Profile**.
    2. Click **Upload Logo** in the Logo section.
    3. Select your PNG or JPG file from your device.
    4. Use the crop and zoom controls to adjust how the logo is framed.
    5. Click **Save** to apply the logo across your invoice templates.

    <Tip>
      If your logo looks blurry or pixelated on the invoice preview, try uploading a higher-resolution version. A width of at least 600 px is recommended for retina displays.
    </Tip>

    ### Saving Your Profile

    After filling in all fields, click **Save Profile**. eInvoices immediately applies these details to your invoice templates. Use the **Preview Invoice** button on any draft invoice to verify how your profile information looks to clients.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Branding">
    Invoice branding lets you extend your visual identity beyond just your logo. Customise colours, fonts, footer text, and payment terms so every invoice feels like a natural extension of your brand.

    ### Custom Colours

    eInvoices lets you set two brand colours that are applied to invoice headers, accent lines, and buttons in the client-facing payment portal.

    1. Go to **Settings → Invoice Branding**.
    2. Click the colour swatch next to **Primary Colour** and enter your brand's hex code (for example, `#1A56DB`) or use the colour picker.
    3. Repeat for **Accent Colour**, which is used for secondary elements such as table headers and subtotal lines.
    4. Click **Save Branding** and then **Preview** to confirm the result.

    ```text
    Primary Colour:  #1A56DB   ← used for header background, headings
    Accent Colour:   #E5EDFF   ← used for table row shading, subtotal bar
    ```

    ### Font Choice

    Choose from a curated set of professional fonts to match your brand voice:

    - **Inter** (default) — clean and modern, ideal for tech and creative businesses
    - **Lato** — friendly and approachable, well-suited for service businesses
    - **Merriweather** — classic serif, ideal for legal, finance, or consulting firms
    - **Roboto Mono** — monospace, great for technical or development-focused invoices

    Select your preferred font from the **Invoice Font** dropdown under **Settings → Invoice Branding**.

    ### Footer Text

    Add a custom footer message that appears at the bottom of every invoice. Common uses include:

    - Payment instructions or bank transfer details
    - A thank-you note (for example, *Thank you for your business!*)
    - Legal disclaimers or terms and conditions

    Keep footer text concise — eInvoices truncates content beyond 300 characters to preserve invoice layout.

    ### Default Payment Terms

    Set the default due date rule applied when you create a new invoice. You can override this on a per-invoice basis at any time.

    | Option | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Net 7** | Payment due 7 days after the invoice date |
    | **Net 15** | Payment due 15 days after the invoice date |
    | **Net 30** | Payment due 30 days after the invoice date |
    | **Due on Receipt** | Payment expected immediately upon delivery |

    Select your preferred default under **Settings → Invoice Branding → Default Payment Terms**, then click **Save**.

    <Warning>
      Changing the default payment terms does not update the due dates on existing draft invoices — only new invoices created after the change will use the new default. Review any open drafts manually.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Tax & Currency">
    Configure your tax and currency settings once so that eInvoices automatically applies the correct rates and symbols to every invoice you create.

    ### Default Tax Rate

    1. Go to **Settings → Tax & Currency**.
    2. Enter your **Default Tax Rate** as a percentage (for example, `20` for 20% VAT or `10` for 10% GST).
    3. Enter your **Tax Name** — this label appears on the invoice line item. Common values include:

        - `VAT` (Value Added Tax — common in the UK and EU)
        - `GST` (Goods and Services Tax — common in Australia, Canada, New Zealand)
        - `Sales Tax` (common in the United States)
        - Any custom label your jurisdiction requires

    4. Click **Save Tax Settings**.

    You can still add, remove, or override the tax rate on individual invoices at any time.

    ### Tax Number Display

    If your jurisdiction requires you to display a tax registration number on invoices (such as a VAT number or ABN):

    1. Enter your registration number in the **Tax Number** field under **Settings → Tax & Currency**.
    2. Toggle **Show Tax Number on Invoices** to on.
    3. Click **Save**. Your tax number will appear beneath your company name in the invoice header.

    <Note>
      If you operate in multiple tax jurisdictions or need compound tax rules (for example, GST + PST in certain Canadian provinces), contact eInvoices Support to enable advanced tax configurations on your account.
    </Note>

    ### Default Currency

    Select the currency used by default on all new invoices:

    1. Under **Settings → Tax & Currency**, open the **Default Currency** dropdown.
    2. Search for and select your currency (for example, **USD — US Dollar**, **EUR — Euro**, **GBP — British Pound**).
    3. Click **Save**.

    ### Multi-Currency Invoicing

    If you bill clients in different currencies, enable multi-currency invoicing to select a different currency per invoice without changing your account default.

    1. Toggle **Enable Multi-Currency Invoicing** to on.
    2. When creating an invoice, use the **Currency** dropdown at the top of the invoice editor to select the currency for that specific invoice.
    3. eInvoices displays all amounts — subtotal, tax, and total — in the selected currency. Exchange rate conversion is not applied automatically; enter the amounts directly in the target currency.

    <Tip>
      Display the exchange rate or conversion note in the **Note to Client** field on multi-currency invoices to give your client full transparency on the billing amount.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Email">
    eInvoices sends invoices, payment reminders, and receipts directly from its delivery infrastructure, but you can personalise the sender experience so every email feels like it comes from your business.

    ### Reply-To Address

    By default, replies to invoice emails go to the eInvoices no-reply address. Set a reply-to address to direct client responses straight to your inbox:

    1. Go to **Settings → Email Settings**.
    2. Enter your preferred **Reply-To Email Address** (for example, `billing@yourcompany.com`).
    3. Click **Save**. All future invoice emails will now direct client replies to this address.

    ### Email Signature

    Add a personal signature that appears at the bottom of every invoice email body:

    1. Click **Edit Signature** under **Settings → Email Settings**.
    2. Use the rich text editor to format your signature. You can include:
        - Your name and title
        - Company name and phone number
        - A website link
        - A short sign-off line
    3. Click **Save Signature**.

    Example signature:

    ```text
    Kind regards,
    Alex Rivera | Accounts & Billing
    Acme Design Studio | +1 (555) 234-5678
    www.acmedesign.com
    ```

    ### CC and BCC Defaults

    Automatically copy additional recipients on every invoice email — useful for keeping an accountant, bookkeeper, or team inbox in the loop.

    | Setting | Use Case |
    |---|---|
    | **Default CC** | Sends a visible copy to the address(es) you specify. Your client can see who was copied. |
    | **Default BCC** | Sends a hidden copy. Useful for internal audit trails without exposing internal addresses to clients. |

    To configure these:

    1. Under **Settings → Email Settings**, enter one or more comma-separated email addresses in the **Default CC** and/or **Default BCC** fields.
    2. Click **Save**.

    You can override or clear these defaults on any individual invoice before sending.

    <Note>
      CC and BCC defaults apply only to invoice delivery emails. Payment confirmation emails and automated reminders use separate notification settings, which you can manage under **Settings → Notifications**.
    </Note>

    ### Sending a Test Email

    After configuring your email settings, click **Send Test Email** to deliver a sample invoice email to your reply-to address. This lets you verify that your signature, reply-to routing, and CC/BCC settings are all working as expected before your next real invoice goes out.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
